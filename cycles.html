Biogeochemical Cycles

Understanding the flow of matter and energy through PyraTerra’s environment

🌋 Carbon Cycle

The Carbon Cycle in PyraTerra revolves around the geothermal release and absorption of carbon. Volcanic vents emit carbon gases that are absorbed by Ember Moss and Cinder Blooms during thermosynthesis. When organisms respire or decompose, carbon is re-released into the atmosphere and recycled back through the molten crust.

🌿 Ember Moss / 🌺 Cinder Bloom (Absorption)

⚡ Nitrogen Cycle

In PyraTerra, lightning storms and geothermal plasma convert atmospheric nitrogen into nitrates usable by thermal plants. Decomposers like Thermobacteria Vulcanis return nitrogen to the atmosphere, while organisms maintain balance by consuming and releasing nitrogen compounds through biological processes.

🌩 Plasma Fixation
→
🌿 Pyra Flora (Nitrate Uptake)
→
🪲 Ember Beetles / 🦎 Lava Lizards (Consumption)
→
🧫 Thermobacteria (Decomposition)

💧 Water Cycle

Despite PyraTerra’s fiery terrain, moisture circulates through geothermal condensation and vapor release. Steam rises from magma vents, condenses into mineral-rich rain, and sustains biotic life. Organisms like the Vapor Bird and Lumin Spores regulate humidity, while underground channels return water to the mantle.
